# Business Model Canvas

## Category
STRATEGIC & DECISION-MAKING

## Description
A strategic management template developed by Alexander Osterwalder for developing new or documenting existing business models. Visualizes 9 key building blocks of how a company creates, delivers, and captures value.

## When to Use
- Launching new business/product
- Pivoting business model
- Strategic planning
- Investor pitches
- Competitive analysis
- Innovation workshops

## Question Sequence (9 questions)

### Question 1/9: CUSTOMER SEGMENTS
Who are you creating value for? What are your most important customer groups?

**Purpose:** Define target market

### Question 2/9: VALUE PROPOSITIONS
What value do you deliver to each customer segment? What problems are you solving? What needs are you satisfying?

**Purpose:** Core offering clarity

### Question 3/9: CHANNELS
Through what channels do your customers want to be reached? How are you reaching them now?

**Purpose:** Distribution strategy

### Question 4/9: CUSTOMER RELATIONSHIPS
What type of relationship does each customer segment expect? How do you acquire, retain, and grow customers?

**Purpose:** Relationship strategy

### Question 5/9: REVENUE STREAMS
For what value are customers willing to pay? How are they currently paying? How would they prefer to pay? (subscription, one-time, usage-based, etc.)

**Purpose:** Monetization model

### Question 6/9: KEY RESOURCES
What key resources do your value propositions require? (physical, intellectual, human, financial)

**Purpose:** Asset requirements

### Question 7/9: KEY ACTIVITIES
What key activities do your value propositions require? What are the most important things you must do?

**Purpose:** Core operations

### Question 8/9: KEY PARTNERSHIPS
Who are your key partners and suppliers? What key resources/activities do partners provide?

**Purpose:** Ecosystem strategy

### Question 9/9: COST STRUCTURE
What are the most important costs? Which key resources and activities are most expensive? (cost-driven vs value-driven)

**Purpose:** Economic model

- Interdependencies identified
- Innovation opportunities spotted
- Risks highlighted
- Investor-ready framework

## Tips
- Use visual canvas (sticky notes)
- Start with customer segments
- Iterate - business models evolve
- Compare to competitors' canvases
- Identify weak links (e.g., great value prop but poor channels)
- Use with Value Proposition Canvas for deeper customer insight
