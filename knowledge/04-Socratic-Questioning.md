# Socratic Questioning (6 Types)

## Category
CRITICAL & SYSTEMS THINKING

## Description
Classical Socratic method with six question types designed to probe thinking deeply and systematically.

## When to Use
- Teaching/mentoring
- Challenging assumptions
- Developing critical thinking
- Therapy (CBT)
- Deep analysis

## Question Sequence (6 questions)

### Question 1/6: CLARIFICATION
What do you mean by...? Can you give an example? Can you rephrase that?

**Purpose:** Ensure clear understanding

### Question 2/6: ASSUMPTIONS
What are you assuming? What could we assume instead? Why would someone assume that?

**Purpose:** Uncover hidden assumptions

### Question 3/6: EVIDENCE/REASONS
How do you know? What evidence supports that? Is there counterevidence?

**Purpose:** Probe for supporting evidence

### Question 4/6: PERSPECTIVES
What's an alternative view? How might others respond? What's the counter-argument?

**Purpose:** Consider different viewpoints

### Question 5/6: IMPLICATIONS
What follows from that? What are the consequences? Then what happens?

**Purpose:** Explore consequences

### Question 6/6: THE QUESTION ITSELF
Why is this question important? What was the point of asking? What does that prove?

**Purpose:** Meta-cognitive reflection

## Expected Outcome
- Deeper understanding
- Exposed assumptions
- Critical thinking developed
- Self-discovered insights

## Tips
- Listen more than speak
- Let silence work
- Build on their answers
- Don't provide answers
