# Appreciative Inquiry (4D Model)

## Category
STRATEGIC & DECISION-MAKING

## Description
A strength-based approach to organizational change. Instead of problem-solving, you focus on discovering what works well and building on it.

## When to Use
- Organizational change
- Team building
- Strategic planning
- Positive culture shift
- When deficit-focused approaches have failed

## Question Sequence (4 questions)

### Question 1/4: DISCOVERY - What gives life?
When have we been at our best? What are our core strengths? Tell a story of a peak experience.

**Purpose:** Appreciate the best of what is

### Question 2/4: DREAM - What might be?
If we built on our strengths, what could we become? What's our ideal future?

**Purpose:** Envision possibilities

### Question 3/4: DESIGN - What should be?
What structures/processes would support our ideal state? What are our "provocative propositions"?

**Purpose:** Co-construct the ideal

### Question 4/4: DESTINY - What will be?
How will we make it happen? Who will do what? How will we sustain this?

**Purpose:** Implement and sustain

## Expected Outcome
- Positive energy and momentum
- Strength-based strategy
- Shared vision
- Collaborative commitment

## Academic Source
- Cooperrider, D. & Srivastva, S. (1987). "Appreciative Inquiry in Organizational Life"
- Case Western Reserve University

## Tips
- Focus on successes, not problems
- Tell stories, don't just list strengths
- Involve diverse stakeholders
- Build on positive core
