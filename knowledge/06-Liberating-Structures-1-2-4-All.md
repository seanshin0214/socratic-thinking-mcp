# Liberating Structures: 1-2-4-All

## Category
CREATIVE & INNOVATION

## Description
A progressive engagement technique that quickly surfaces insights from groups by moving from individual reflection, to pairs, to small groups, to whole group sharing. Ensures all voices are heard and builds on collective intelligence.

## When to Use
- Group brainstorming sessions
- Need diverse perspectives quickly
- Surfacing hidden insights
- Building on collective wisdom
- Engaging quiet participants
- Time-constrained ideation

## Question Sequence (4 questions)

### Question 1/4: INDIVIDUAL (1 minute)
What is your immediate response to [challenge/question]? Write down your first thoughts.

**Purpose:** Individual reflection without group influence

### Question 2/4: PAIRS (2 minutes)
Share your thoughts with a partner. What patterns or new insights emerge when you compare notes?

**Purpose:** Build on ideas through dialogue

### Question 3/4: FOURSOMES (4 minutes)
Join with another pair (now group of 4). What are the most important themes or breakthrough ideas from your discussions?

**Purpose:** Identify strongest insights through aggregation

### Question 4/4: ALL (5 minutes)
Each group shares one key insight. What patterns emerge across all groups? What surprises you?

**Purpose:** Collective synthesis and discovery

## Expected Outcome
- Multiple perspectives surfaced quickly (15-20 min total)
- Quiet voices included
- Patterns and themes identified
- Actionable insights prioritized
- Group alignment on key ideas

## Academic Source
- Lipmanowicz, H. & McCandless, K. (2013). "The Surprising Power of Liberating Structures"
- www.liberatingstructures.com
- Used by organizations worldwide (Google, Red Cross, UN)

## Tips
- Strict time limits (1-2-4 minutes)
- Move quickly between phases
- Don't debate, just capture
- Look for surprising agreements
- One mic rule for final sharing
