# Question Storming

## Category
CREATIVE & INNOVATION

## Description
Instead of brainstorming ideas, you brainstorm questions. This technique reframes problems, uncovers assumptions, and opens new solution spaces before jumping to answers.

## When to Use
- Complex problems with unclear definition
- Need to challenge assumptions
- Team stuck in solution mode
- Early stage innovation/design
- Reframing existing challenges

## Question Sequence (7 questions)

### Question 1/7: WHAT IS?
What is the current situation? What facts do we know?

**Purpose:** Establish baseline understanding

### Question 2/7: WHAT CAUSED?
What caused this situation? What are the root factors?

**Purpose:** Identify underlying causes

### Question 3/7: WHAT IF?
What if [constraint] didn't exist? What if we had unlimited resources/time/budget?

**Purpose:** Remove mental limitations

### Question 4/7: WHY?
Why does this matter? Why should anyone care about solving this?

**Purpose:** Clarify significance and motivation

### Question 5/7: WHO?
Who is affected? Who has solved similar problems? Who else should we ask?

**Purpose:** Identify stakeholders and perspectives

### Question 6/7: HOW MIGHT WE?
How might we [reframed problem]? Generate 10+ different "How might we..." questions

**Purpose:** Reframe problem in actionable ways

### Question 7/7: WHAT'S MISSING?
What questions haven't we asked? What are we avoiding? What blind spots might we have?

**Purpose:** Uncover hidden assumptions

## Expected Outcome
- 50+ questions generated
- Problem reframed from multiple angles
- Assumptions surfaced and challenged
- New solution spaces identified

## Academic Source
- Gregersen, H. (2018). "Questions Are the Answer: A Breakthrough Approach to Your Most Vexing Problems at Work and in Life"
- Harvard Business Review articles on innovation questioning

## Tips
- Aim for quantity (50+ questions)
- No answers allowed during session
- Wildcard questions encouraged
- Group questions by type after generation
